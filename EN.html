Digital Transformation: Website & App Revamp

Project timeline3 months
Team size6 vendors & 4 departments

💡 Marketing-led design decisions

Highlighting "great value for money"
Goal: Make our value-for-money advantage visible to more customers.
Action: Cross-department alignment (merchandising) + leadership approval to update legacy processes.
Result: New system fully displays prices, strengthening our value image and boosting conversion.
Richer visuals, better engagement
Goal: Increase time on site and purchase intent.
Action: Introduced immersive product hero images, replacing text-heavy layouts.
Result: Fresh visual identity, clearer product features, significantly higher browse depth.

Cross-department product classification

Marketing and product teams had conflicting taxonomies. I visualized the gaps with a mind map, facilitated workshops to align both sides, and built a unified template balancing customer experience and inventory management.

Example: Kids' shoes
• Switched from "product category" to "age-based".
• Parents can quickly find the right product for their children.
• Result: Greatly reduced drop-off.

System migration crisis

Situations

Vendor staff shortage delayed API delivery; critical gap risk over Christmas & New Year during legacy system switch-off.

Solutions
  • Proposed a "phased launch" plan approved by leadership, reducing single-switchover risk.
  • Communicated with 3 legacy vendors to arrange a short-term backup extension, ensuring business continuity during transition.
  • Authored temporary POS guides for frontline stores to keep offline operations smooth.
Outcome: Phase 1 went live on time, store operations transitioned seamlessly.
